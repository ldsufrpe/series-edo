Assim, é possível perceber que a soma parcial de \(N\) termos é:
\begin{align*} S_N = \amp\sum_{n=5}^N \Big(\log(n-3)-\log(n)\Big)\\ \amp\hskip0.5in=\log 2+\log 3 + \log 4 - \log(N-2)-\log(N-1)-\log(N)\\ \amp\hskip0.5in=\log \left(\frac{24}{N(N-1)(N-2)}\right)\\ \end{align*}

para \(N \geq 7\text{.}\) Então,

\begin{align*} \sum_{n=5}^\infty \Big(\log(n-3)-\log(n)\Big)\amp= \lim_{N \to \infty}S_N\\ \amp=\lim_{N \to \infty}\log \left(\frac{24}{N(N-1)(N-2)}\right)\\ \amp=-\infty \end{align*}
