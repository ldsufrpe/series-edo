Let \(\sum\limits_{n=0}^\infty A_n (x-c)^n\) be a power series. Then one of the following alternatives must hold.

  1. The power series converges for every number \(x\text{.}\) In this case we say that the radius of convergence is \(\infty\text{.}\)

  2. There is a number \(0 \lt R \lt \infty\) such that the series converges for \(|x-c| \lt R\) and diverges for \(|x-c| \gt R\text{.}\) Then \(R\) is called the radius of convergence.

  3. The series converges for \(x=c\) and diverges for all \(x\ne c\text{.}\) In this case, we say that the radius of convergence is \(0\text{.}\)
