Solução 1.5.8.1.

Já que \(\displaystyle \ln(1-x) = - \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{x^n}n\text{,}\) também temos, portanto, \(\displaystyle \ln(1+x) = - \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(-x)^n}n\text{.}\) Assim

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} 9 x \ln(1+x) \amp = -9 \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(-x)^n x}n \\\\ \amp = 9 \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(-x)^{n+1}}n \\\\ \amp = \frac{9 x^2}1 - \frac{9 x^3}2 + \frac{9 x^4}3 - \cdots \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

e daí \(c_0 = 0\text{,}\) \(c_1 = 0\text{,}\) \(c_2 = 9\text{,}\) \(c_3 = \frac{-9}2 = -4.5\text{,}\) \(c_4 = \frac{9}3 = 3\text{,}\) and in general, \(c_n = \frac{9(-1)^n}{n-1}\) for all \(n \geq 2\text{.}\)
