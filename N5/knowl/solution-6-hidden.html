Seja

\begin{equation*} f(x)=e^{-2x},\;f'(x)=-2e^{-2x},\; f''(x)=4e^{-2x}\text{.} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(0)=1,\;f'(0)=-2,\; f''(0)=4 \end{equation*}

Nós podemos ver isso

\begin{equation*} f^{(k)}(0)=(-2)^k \end{equation*}

de modo que a série de Maclaurin é

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle =\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\frac{(-2)^k x^k}{k!} \end{equation*}
