Parágrafo
\begin{equation*} f(0)=\ln(7),\;f'(0)={\textstyle\frac{1}{7}},\; f''(0)=-{\textstyle\frac{1}{49}},\; f'''(0)={\textstyle\frac{2}{343}} \end{equation*}

Podemos ver isso para \(k\geq 1\)

\begin{equation*} f^{(k)}(0)=\frac{(-1)^{k-1}\cdot(k-1)!}{(7)^k} \end{equation*}

de modo que a série de Maclaurin é

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle =\ln(7)+\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^{k-1} x^k}{(7)^k k} \end{equation*}
