Solution 1: Using the ideas of Question [cross-reference to target(s) "prob_s3_2_cookies2" missing or not unique], we see:
\begin{align*} \sum_{n={50}}^{100} \frac{1}{5^n}&=\sum_{n=0}^{100}\frac{1}{5^n}-\sum_{n=0}^{49}\frac{1}{5^n}\\ \end{align*}

That is, we want start with the sum of all the terms up to \(\dfrac{1}{5^{100}}\text{,}\) and then subtract off the ones we actually don't want, which is everything up to \(\dfrac{1}{5^{49}}\text{.}\) Now, both series are in a form appropriate for Lemma [cross-reference to target(s) "lem_geomsum" missing or not unique].

\begin{align*} \sum_{n=0}^{100}\frac{1}{5^n}-\sum_{n=0}^{49}\frac{1}{5^n}&=\frac{1-\frac{1}{5^{101}}}{1-\frac15}-\frac{1-\frac{1}{5^{50}}}{1-\frac15}\\ &=\frac{5^{101}-1}{4\cdot 5^{100}}-\frac{5^{50}-1}{4\cdot 5^{49}}\left(\frac{5^{51}}{5^{51}}\right)\\ &=\frac{5^{101}-1}{4\cdot 5^{100}}-\frac{5^{101}-5^{51}}{4\cdot 5^{100}}\\ &=\frac{5^{51}-1}{4\cdot 5^{100}} \end{align*}
