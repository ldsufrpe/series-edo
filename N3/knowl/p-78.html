Comparison Test and Limit Comparison Test

  • works well when, for very large \(n\text{,}\) the \(n^{\mathrm{th}}\) term \(a_n\) is approximately the same as a simpler term \(b_n\) (see Example Exemplo 1.10) and it is easy to determine whether or not \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty b_n\) converges

  • don't forget to check that \(b_n\ge 0\)

  • usually the Limit Comparison Test is easier to apply than the Comparison Test
