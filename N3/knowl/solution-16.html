Solução 1.9.19.1.

Com \(a_n = \left(1+\frac{1}{2 n}\right)^{-n^2}\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \sqrt[n]{a_n} = \sqrt[n]{\left(1+\frac{1}{2 n}\right)^{-n^2}} = \left( 1 + \frac{1}{2 n} \right)^{-n} \end{equation*}

e

\begin{equation*} \lim_{n \to \infty} \sqrt[n]{a_n} = \lim_{n\to\infty} \left( 1+\frac{1}{2 n}\right)^{-n} = \lim_{n\to\infty}\left[\left(1+\frac{1}{2 n} \right)^{2 n} \right]^{-\frac{1}{2}} =e^{-\frac{1}{2}}\lt 1. \end{equation*}

Portanto, a série \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty} \left(1+\frac{1}{2 n}\right)^{-n^2}\) converge pelo Teste de Raiz.
